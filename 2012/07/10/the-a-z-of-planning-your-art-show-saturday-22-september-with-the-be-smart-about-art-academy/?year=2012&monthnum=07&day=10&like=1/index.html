The A-Z of Planning Your Art Show – Saturday 22 September with the Be Smart About Art Academy

Posted on July 10, 2012 by Susan Mumford

This four-hour workshop led by Susan Mumford and a Guest Presenter takes artists and early-stage curators through all stages of an exhibition, from start to finish. It is a comprehensive session that aims to give participants essential tips and guidance, provides each person with guidelines to takeaway and furthermore invites each attendee to finish with a unique exhibition action plan. 

We will cover:

•Exhibition options (solo or group with or without an agent, artist collective, curated, juried exhibition, rented gallery, pop-up space, open studio, artist fair);
•Setting your budget and creating an exhibition plan; 
•Sponsorship or funding opportunities (alcohol sponsor, ACE grant, crowd funding campaign);
•Critical consideration of location (passing trade, suitable area?);
•Reviewing gallery hire or representing gallery offers and terms (is everything included and fair?) and negotiating if applicable;
•Consideration of dates (bank holidays, school holidays, lead time etc);
•Events to be held during show;
•Deciding title and supportive statement;
•Selecting / creating art;
•Marketing & Press campaign (online and offline – website, email campaign, art websites, facebook invite, twitter, linked in, press release to publications, listings, business cards, postal invitations, letters to clients etc);
•Press Release basics (and press aims, if applicable);
•Presentation of art (frames, stands, print rack, plastic sleeves);
•Pricing of art (key points and frames/presentation);
•Exhibition materials (labels, visitors’ book, takeaway materials such as press release or overview);
•Brochure/catalogues/invites, price list, numbers for walls;
•Installation (curating, how to install, archival considerations, lighting);
•Preparing to sell: Invoice, Payment, Discounts, Delivery/Installation;
•Preparing and holding events (beverages, staff, hours – and so on);
•Selling top tips: Conversations in the exhibition, call to action, how to follow-up;
•Follow-up sales (offering viewings and what to have prepared);
•Following exhibition: Adding people to mailing list, follow-up email, facebook, twitter;
•Exhibition review.
…all in 4 hours!

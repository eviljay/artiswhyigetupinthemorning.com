Recap of an art show I opened — ‘Home: Contemporary Female Masters’

Posted on October 16, 2013 by Susan Mumford

On Friday 11th October 2013, I had the honour of being the opening speaker for an exhibition at the Bulgarian Cultural Institute, London, the show of which was curated by London-based Bulgarian artist Paola Minekov. Titled ‘Home: Contemporary Female Masters’, the exposition featured recent works by seven women artists:

Caroline Underwood, Diana Ali, Malika Sqalli, Minna George, Tamar Lev-On, Tina Mammoser and Paola Minekov.

Paola writes of the show:
“The exhibition, Home: Contemporary Female Masters, portrays women’s experience of choosing to migrate and build a home in a foreign country. This relatively new social phenomenon is examined in depth by seven mid career international women artists with strong links to Europe, the Middle East, America and Africa. They explore their experience of establishing themselves and building the elements of what they perceive as ‘home’ wherever they are in the world.”

I was invited to open the private view as a figure in the London art world who hails from shores afar, and specifically as Founder of the Association of Women Art Dealers (AWAD).

At the opening, I invited the audience to consider how the art on display and the exhibitors’ practices in general have been informed by their global journeys. Further to this, the individuals’ developed identitites have been influenced by original cultural heritage, yet have grown as residents of a larger world, who stand independently of a single heritage. The Bulgarian Cultural Institute provided a home for these works of art resulting from a new, shared identity.

A quotation by Margaret Mead comes to mind: “If we are to achieve a richer culture, rich in contrasting values, we must recognise the whole gamut of human potentialities, and so we are a less arbitrary social fabric, one in which each diverse gift will find a fitting place.”
